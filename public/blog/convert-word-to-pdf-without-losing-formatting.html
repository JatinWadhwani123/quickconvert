How to Convert Word to PDF Without Losing Formatting

Converting a Word document to PDF is common when sharing resumes, contracts, reports, or official documents. However, many users experience formatting issues such as shifted text, broken spacing, missing fonts, or misaligned images. This guide explains how to convert Word to PDF without losing formatting.

Why Formatting Changes When Converting Word to PDF

Formatting issues usually occur because:

  • Fonts are not embedded
  • Different software versions are used
  • Margins are incorrectly set
  • Images are not properly anchored
  • Custom styles are unsupported

Using a reliable conversion method prevents these problems.

Tips to Preserve Formatting

1. Use Standard Fonts

Fonts like Arial, Times New Roman, and Calibri are safer and widely supported.

2. Fix Margins Before Converting

Check margins and spacing before exporting.

3. Embed Images Properly

Ensure images are aligned and set to “In line with text” in Word.

4. Save as PDF from Word (Alternative Method)

You can also use “Save As → PDF” inside Microsoft Word.
