How to Compress PDF for Email (Step-by-Step Guide)

Sending a PDF through email can be frustrating when the file size is too large. Many email services like Gmail and Outlook limit attachments to 20–25MB. If your PDF exceeds that limit, your email may fail to send. Fortunately, compressing a PDF file is simple and only takes a few minutes.

Why Large PDFs Fail in Email

Email providers limit attachment sizes to prevent spam and reduce server load. High-resolution images, scanned documents, and embedded fonts often increase PDF file size. If your PDF is too large, the email system will block it automatically.

What Is the Ideal PDF Size for Email?

For most email services:

  • Gmail: 25MB maximum
  • Outlook: 20MB maximum
  • Corporate email servers: Often 10–15MB

To ensure smooth delivery, it’s best to keep your PDF under 10MB.

How PDF Compression Works

PDF compression reduces file size by:

  • Optimizing embedded images
  • Removing unnecessary metadata

Tips to Keep PDF Size Small

  • Use lower image resolution when scanning documents
  • Avoid embedding unnecessary fonts
  • Remove unused pages
  • Compress before sharing

Frequently Asked Questions

How much can a PDF be compressed?

Compression levels depend on the content, but many PDFs can be reduced by 30–70%.

Will compression reduce quality?

Light compression maintains nearly identical quality. Heavy compression may slightly reduce image clarity.

Can I compress scanned PDFs?

Yes. Scanned PDFs often benefit the most from compression due to large embedded images.
